Rebecca Armstrong

Music Researcher | Mathematics Student | Dance Anthropologist | Educational Advocate

About Me

Rebecca Armstrong is an Irish Choreomundus master’s scholar with a multidisciplinary academic background in musicology, mathematics, dance, anthropology, and education. She holds a First Class Joint Honours BA in Mathematics and Music from Trinity College Dublin (TCD). She has been awarded TCD’s Gold Medal for exceptional academic performance and won multiple prizes for her research.

Research & Publications

  • Why Musicologists Don’t Dance: An award-winning undergraduate musicology dissertation.
  • Digitally Dancing in Defiance: An ethnographic study of the United Ukrainian Ballet.
  • Back to Our Roots: Research on ecological consciousness through eco-literate music education.
  • Master’s Dissertation: Investigating flow experiences in flamenco classrooms.

Projects & Initiatives

As an advocate for educational development, Rebecca served as an educational development officer for the STAND charity society, where she developed initiatives to integrate arts education into disadvantaged schools.

Dance & Creative Work

Rebecca is a trained ballet dancer with experience in contemporary, afro-contemporary, and choreography. She has worked in sound engineering for theatre and is exploring documentary filmmaking.

Technical Skills & Internships

Rebecca has experience in machine learning, Python, R, and IT architecture. She completed an internship at RITMO (University of Oslo) and worked in Cloud IT Architecture at Vodafone in 2021.
